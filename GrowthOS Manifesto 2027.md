## The Tension

### The widest gap in business is between what customers want and what companies make.

The cost of being wrong just spiked. Capital is expensive, tariffs are unstable, the customer is walking. **70%** of Americans say they're less brand loyal than a year ago.[^1] **95%** of enterprise GenAI pilots have delivered zero measurable P&L impact against $30–40B in spend.[^2] Year-one product mortality runs **70–90%** across every major industry, unchanged in a generation.[^3] Companies have more telemetry on the customer than at any point in history. The customer is leaving anyway.

The gap isn't a data problem. The gap is a *bet* problem. Look at any product that missed its market, in any industry, and the bet behind it landed in one of three conventional patterns.

## Three Conventional Bets

### 01 — The myopic bet.

The idea came from one person's office. It reflects what they noticed, who they talked to, what their team had been obsessing over for the last quarter. It looks defensible inside the building until it meets the customer it was meant for. Then it dies.

**Apple · Project Titan.** Built for an Apple version of mobility the mobility market never asked for. Cancelled February 2024.
- 2014–2024 · ~$10B spent · 2,000+ engineers at peak · 600+ laid off at cancellation

**Humane · AI Pin.** Founders' conviction in wearable AI. Returns exceeded sales within months of the $699 launch.
- $230M raised · Launched Apr 2024 · Sold to HP for $116M (Feb 2025) · Subscription required: $24/mo

**Amazon × Berkshire × JPMorgan · Haven.** Three CEOs' theory of how to fix US healthcare. Dissolved with no shipped product.
- 2018–2021 · Atul Gawande, CEO · 0 products launched · 1.2M employees covered in scope

**CNN · CNN+.** A premium streaming news product nobody had asked for. Killed 30 days after launch.
- Mar 29 → Apr 28, 2022 · $300M+ spent pre-launch · ~10K DAU at peak · 350+ jobs cut

### 02 — The incremental bet.

A 3% feature added to a product already missing the 30% the customer needed in the first place. The team is too busy keeping the boat in its lane to notice the lane is empty. Innovation budgets get spent defending the existing roadmap from the customer's actual reality.

**US banks · AI chatbots.** Conversational AI bolted into mobile apps while fraud-dispute hold times still run 30–50+ minutes.
- $17B JPM tech spend (2024) · Erica, Cora, et al. · Wireline NPS at decade lows · Hold-time complaints up YoY

**Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday · Agentic Copilot.** AI agents bolted onto existing UIs while integration sprawl, configuration cost, and license complexity sit untouched.
- Agentforce launched Sept 2024 · Avg enterprise: 100+ SaaS apps · $30–40B GenAI spend, 95% zero P&L · Customer NPS at multi-year lows

**Epic, Cerner (Oracle Health) · AI scribe summaries.** GPT-powered note summaries shipped 2023–24. Physician burnout still at record highs.
- 63% MD burnout (AMA, 2024) · ~75% of US hospital EHRs · Abridge: $250M raised (2024) · Suki, Ambience taking share

**Verizon, AT&T · 5G+ marketing tiers.** "Next-gen" tier marketing while billing complexity and coverage reliability stay broken.
- ACSI wireless at 10-yr lows · Postpaid ARPU up 12–15% YoY · Mid-band 5G still spotty in metros · Verizon shed 68K postpaid (Q1 '24)

### 03 — The extractive bet.

Shrinkflation. Fee creep. Subscription stacking. Tier-stripping. Prior-authorization expansion. The "innovation" pipeline that ships only what improves the bottom line and not what changes what customers actually get. Customers can feel it, and they're saying so out loud.

**BMW · Heated-seat subscription.** Monthly fee to use heated seats already installed in the car. Backlash forced a rollback in 2023.
- $18/mo or $180/yr · Launched July 2022 · Rolled back Sept 2023 · Other features still gated

**Mercedes-Benz · Acceleration unlock.** $1,200/year subscription to unlock 0–60 acceleration on EQ models customers had already paid for.
- $1,200/yr "Acceleration Increase" · EQE 0–60: 6.0s → 5.1s when paid · Still active in 2024 · Active class-action filings

**Ford, GM, Tesla · Driver-assist as service.** Hardware-already-paid-for driver-assist gated behind recurring fees.
- Ford BlueCruise: $75/mo after 3yr · GM Super Cruise: $25/mo after 3yr · Tesla FSD: $99/mo or $8K · All same hardware regardless

**Major streamers · Price hikes + ad tiers + password crackdowns.** Raised prices, added ads, cracked down on sharing, all at once. Cancellations hit industry records.
- 24M+ US cancellations (2024) · Disney+ −4M subs after hike · 5–6% monthly churn vs 2–3% (2022) · Netflix sharing crackdown: May 2023

**US airlines · Basic economy.** A smaller product, repackaged and sold for the same money. Ancillary revenue per passenger up sharply.
- +50% ancillary/pax since 2020 · 35–40% of bookings on some routes · DOT moves to ban worst practices (2024) · Bag/seat/boarding fees stack

**UnitedHealth & major insurers · Prior authorization.** Expanded denials to the point that denial-as-product became the news story of late 2024.
- 2.7B PA requests (2023, KFF) · UHC ~9% denial rate (highest) · Multiple state laws 2024–25 · Public backlash post-Dec 2024

**Adobe, Autodesk · Annual price hikes.** Year-over-year increases. Whole markets defected to Figma, Affinity, and Blender.
- Creative Cloud: +15–25% (2020–24) · Adobe–Figma blocked Dec 2023 ($20B) · Affinity acquired by Canva (2024) · Blender user base +50%+ same period

## The Conviction Bet

### And then there's the bet that closes the gap.

The bet that closes the gap shares three ingredients every time. Data on the table that makes the case defensible. A narrative sharp enough to convince an audience who can fund it. A specific person willing to put themselves on the record before the outcome is known. The three conventional bets miss because one or more of those ingredients is missing. The conviction bet does not.

**Netflix · Hastings · Streaming pivot.** Bet broadband would kill the DVD before DVD revenue did. Survived a 75% stock collapse in 2011 when investors balked at the Qwikster split. Kept pouring capital into content while the bet remained unproven.
- $19.1B streaming content obligations (Q3 2020) · $17B+ annual content spend by early 2020s · 75% stock loss absorbed (2011) · Stock 2010 → 2021: $10 → $600+ (split-adjusted)

**Figma · Field · Browser-native design.** Bet designers wanted real-time collaboration in the browser, not desktop apps and file emails. Stayed in stealth for four years. Survived Adobe's attempt to absorb them and outlasted the deal.
- ~$330M raised across 9 years · 4 years in stealth (2012–2016) · Adobe $20B acquisition blocked Dec 2023 · Valuation: $0 → $12.5B (2024 secondary)

**NVIDIA · Huang · The CUDA bet.** Bet GPUs would matter for compute, not just graphics. Spent fifteen years building CUDA through analyst skepticism before the AI boom proved the bet right.
- 15+ years of sustained CUDA R&D · Market cap 2014 → 2024: ~$10B → $3T+ · $30B+ annual R&D by 2024 · Powers ~80% of AI training workloads

**Best Buy · Joly · Renew Blue.** Doubled down on stores when every analyst said close them. Matched online prices and ate the margin hit. Turned the showroom into the answer to showrooming.
- $1B annual margin pressure from price-matching · $1.5B+ in store and supply-chain investment · Stock: $13 → $77 (2012 → 2019) · Market cap recovery: ~$25B

**Stripe · Collisons · Developer-first payment infra.** Bet that developers, not procurement teams, would choose payment infrastructure. Spent a decade competing on docs and API quality before adding sales motion.
- ~$9B raised over 13 years · $95B private valuation (2021 peak) · $1.4T processed in 2024 · ~50% of US adults transacted via Stripe (2023)

> Innovation does not happen because the evidence is good.
> It happens because *a specific person at a specific company decides to take a bet.*
>
> — The tension

Historically, the bets that closed the gap had to invent workarounds to do it. 3M's 15% time, the rule that made the Post-it possible. Sony's PlayStation, which survived a board veto only by being incubated under Sony Music. Bill Hewlett launching the HP-35 calculator over the protest of his own market research. Innovation kept reaching for tricks because the system itself never gave it conviction. GrowthOS gives the conviction back. Confidence the case will hold, before the bet gets placed.

Which means the work of closing the gap is the work of helping that company make *the conviction bet*. Feed them the customer's voice their team would have missed. Surface the market context their incrementalism would have ignored. Generate the argument against the extractive option before someone in finance makes it for them.

Closing the gap is not a feature. It's the work of holding research, analysis, and the workflow in one place, long enough for a team to see the case clearly, make the bet, and ship. That's the promise. One workbench where the customer's voice sits next to the team's plan, from the first signal through the analysis all the way to in-market. Not another tool in the stack. The system that makes the stack unnecessary.

## Audience

### What are the roles of the GrowthOS?

GrowthOS exists to help companies make the conviction bet. The bet has three ingredients: data-backed evidence, a persuasive case, the right audience for it. Each ingredient comes from a different job. The practitioner produces the evidence. The manager runs the portfolio those bets live in. The executive constructs the case that wins the funding.

Each FY27 commitment is a piece of the system those jobs need, so when the bet gets made it's a conviction bet, not a conventional one.

### Practitioner — *The person doing the work.*

Where ideation actually happens. Researchers, designers, product strategists, account leaders shaping concepts and gathering the evidence to validate them. They live in the Idea Container, the Insight Map, the experiment setup. They need flow, not friction. Every artifact they create should travel upstream automatically.

> **Without this job:** the bet goes **myopic**. Defensible inside the building, dead when it meets the customer.

### Manager — *The person running the portfolio.*

Holds a stable of innovation efforts. Needs operational truth about all of them at once, without forcing their teams to do separate reporting work. Status, evidence, blockers, next gates. The view should emerge as a real-time function of what's happening in the system underneath, not as a slide deck assembled on Friday afternoon.

> **Without this job:** the bet goes **incremental**. Teams defend the existing roadmap and never see the customer-shaped hole.

### Executive — *The person making the case upward.*

Owns the strategy. Their job is to walk into a room and convince someone with power to fund the bets that matter. Today they live in slides and gut. Tomorrow they live in a system designed to help them construct the argument: mapping evidence to known objections, flagging soft spots, generating counter-arguments to defeat before someone else makes them.

> **Without this job:** the bet goes **extractive**. Finance pre-drafted that case, and nobody else built a stronger one.

## Our Principles

### We're adding a new guiding principle to shape FY27.

Three principles carry forward from prior manifestos. Sharpened, but the spirit holds. A fourth, AI in Service of Judgment, is new, and it pushes against the grain of every other AI product in market. We treat it separately because it deserves the space.

### Principle 01 — Elevate Ideas

Every feature should expand and enhance ideas. Our tools must make ideas clearer, more actionable, and visibly better with each iteration.

- **Insight vs. Data.** We prioritize meaningful synthesis over raw information. GrowthOS should help users extract what matters most, not just collect more.
- **Human Agency.** Tools should enhance human decision-making, not replace it. GrowthOS reinforces the user's voice, judgment, and creativity.
- **Intuitive Experiences.** Using GrowthOS should feel natural and fluid: each input, idea, insight, and piece of research driving and connecting to the next possible step.

### Principle 02 — Intelligent Growth

The system gets smarter as it's used, and honest about where it isn't yet. Inputs sharpen patterns. Patterns reveal gaps. Gaps become the next questions.

- **Compounding intelligence.** Every project leaves residue. Every test sharpens the next one. Foundations learns what this client cares about, what's already been tried, what the next bet should weigh.
- **Honest sketchiness.** A solid rectangle is evidence. A dashed one is a hypothesis. A '?' is a known unknown. The system surfaces what it doesn't know so the user can do something about it.
- **Validated evidence.** Decisions are driven by experimentation, robust insights, and measurable outcomes. The UDL carries the residue forward. Confidence is earned, not assumed.

### Principle 03 — Extensible & Flexible Architecture

We don't compete with the tools. We sell the behavioral and strategic knowledge that turns inputs into guidance. When something accelerates client value, we integrate it. When the novel pieces don't exist yet, we build them ourselves: the interfaces that shape understanding and human agency.

- **Best tool for the job.** Designed to integrate with diverse external tools and pick the strongest one for each moment. We don't build everything ourselves; we build the system that turns signals into actions.
- **Collaboration.** Frictionless collaborative experiences. Getting started and working together should feel as effortless as any mainstream tool.

### Principle 04 (new) — AI in Service of Judgment

The grain of most AI products today is to do the work for the user. The signals are clear about where that leads. Brain capacity lowers, creative power decreases, shortcuts replace mental challenges. Having AI do the thinking for you is a first-class ticket to mediocrity for life.

GrowthOS makes the opposite bet. AI here is a mental coach. It knows when to push and when to pause. How to activate the brain's creative and problem-solving networks. Priming, gamification, behavioral design, flow inducement, sentiment coaching. We use AI to make the human sharper, not lazier. *Plus est en vous.* There is more in you.

- **01 Commit first.** User names their hypothesis or writes their first draft before AI is allowed to intervene. AI then pushes back.
- **02 Propose, don't generate.** The failure mode of AI is the user becoming a passenger. GrowthOS treats every AI move as a proposal: fast enough to save the cold-start cost of drafting, slow enough to leave judgment in human hands. AI proposes; the user judges.
- **03 Long tail first.** AI surfaces the outlier, the unusual quote, the response that doesn't fit. The interesting answer is rarely the average.
- **04 Adversarial by default.** AI generates the strongest argument against what the user is building, so the user can defeat it before someone else does.
- **05 Slow at decisions.** Ingest is fast. Judgment is deliberate. Friction at the thinking points is a design feature.

## What We're Building — FY27

The commitments below are what GrowthOS will be by year-end. Each one is a stake in the ground about what the system has to become, not which screen or release will get there. Each will get its own working prototype to make the direction concrete before it ships.

### 01 — Make Growthmentor the conversational coach.

Every conversation with Growthmentor exists to make the user sharper. Each one proposes a reading the user can take or reshape. Each one surfaces an angle the team would have missed. Each one pushes back when the case is thin. The user thinks harder for having had it. The work is still theirs.

Underneath the conversation, Growthmentor is the orchestrator. A well-designed system of specialized LLMs and tools coordinated by the central core, your project. It picks the best tool for the job, whether that's OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, or whatever comes next. The intelligence is in the architecture, not any single model. GrowthOS stays LLM-agnostic on purpose.

The result is one always-on character. Proposing, judging-aloud, honest about what it doesn't know. There to make the human sharper, not lazier.

### 02 — Make activation the strongest moment in the product.

The first hour is where every enterprise platform loses its users. Forms, picklists, empty repositories, "now what?" GrowthOS reframes activation as the highest-leverage design surface we own. A user types a brief. Growthmentor proposes a structural read. An insight map fills in turn by turn, anchored to sources where they exist and explicitly marked sketchy where they don't. By the time the opening conversation ends, the user has a living artifact, not a static brief.

### 03 — Build for the manager and the executive as deliberately as for the practitioner.

Practitioners have GrowthOS today. Managers and executives see slivers of it. That changes this year. Operational truth about every project, in real time, no Friday slide deck. Drill-into-any-project views that surface evidence, blockers, gates, owners. Portfolio-level patterns that show where bets are concentrated, where evidence is thin. Case-building for the moment of upward persuasion: map evidence to objections, surface counterarguments before someone else makes them, walk into the room with the case already constructed.

### 04 — Make collaboration effortless and continuous.

No bolt-on. The same surface adapts to multiple people working at once. Activity flows up automatically. Comments, mentions, decisions, evidence reviews, all attached to the artifact they pertain to, not scattered across Slack, email, and decks. Every change has an audit trail: who added what, when, and why. Getting started and working together should feel as effortless as any mainstream tool.

### 05 — Make Foundations a live research stack.

Real connectors (Alphasense, web research, Accenture Thought Leadership, client internal systems). Drag-and-drop ingestion of decks, transcripts, PDFs. Structured data integrations with provenance preserved. Deep-research agents that run multi-step plans across multiple sources. Auditable end-to-end, so when the executive makes the case upward they can answer 'how do you know' without scrambling.

### 06 — Make the UDL the way GrowthOS grows smarter.

The moat we sell is combination. The moat that lasts is compounding. The Unified Data Layer is how everything ever learned in GrowthOS gets carried forward. A survey panel becomes a pattern library. A failed Proxi becomes a guardrail. A successful bet becomes a precedent. Surfaced when relevant. Hidden when not. Sharpened with use. Generic AI has no memory of your last project. GrowthOS does.

### 07 — Win the depth fight inside every named feature.

The combination of research, analysis, and workflow is the moat. But the combination only holds if every feature inside it can stand against the standalone competitor in its category. Activation, collaboration, the UDL, Foundations: each has to feel right against the best in market. Parity at minimum. Leadership where we can.

## The Opportunity

### The category has not been built yet.

Three categories of tool each cover a sliver of the work. Research platforms own evidence-gathering. Analysis platforms own quantitative truth. Workflow tools own the work, with no native research or data layer. None of them combine all three. None of them carries a POV about what good growth work actually looks like.

GrowthOS does. We bring *research, analysis, and the workflow* into one place, and we have the conviction about how the work should go that turns the combination from a kit into a system. That's the moat. That's the category. We will own it, internally at Accenture and externally through scalable SaaS offerings.

### The moat we sell is combination. The moat that lasts is compounding.

Standing up research, analysis, and workflow with a point of view is the table-stakes argument. It's true, and it closes generic AI tools out, but only as a snapshot. What makes GrowthOS structurally defensible is what happens to that combination over time.

Every project leaves residue. A survey panel becomes a pattern library. A failed Proxi becomes a guardrail. A successful bet becomes a precedent. A counterargument that defeated a previous case becomes a prompt that surfaces on the next one. The Unified Data Layer holds it all. Foundations feeds it. Growthmentor reaches into it on every turn.

Generic AI has no memory of your last project. GrowthOS does. The longer it's used, the smarter the starting position gets, for the next project, for the next user, for the next client in the same industry.

### Why us?

Commercial AI models will always be generic. No matter how many documents get uploaded, the model has no focus, no context, no relationship to the work being done. The differentiator is the institutional intelligence behind the system.

GrowthOS puts that intelligence to work. Not as documents AI scrapes, but as the system inside which AI is asked to think. The people who built the system know the work. The system carries their judgment forward.

Everybody can build a tool. Closing the gap between customers and companies is a different game.
